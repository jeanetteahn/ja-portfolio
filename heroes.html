Heroes

A privacy-preserving healthcare monitoring system designed to reduce caregiver emotional burden while enabling early detection of health crises. Awarded Top 3 in the Healthtech track at a Tech Innovation Jam.

Role UX Researcher & Content Writer
Project Type Tech Innovation Jam - Healthtech
Timeline September - November 2024
Recognition Top 3 Finalist - Healthtech Track

Project Brief

Heroes was developed during a Tech Innovation Jam as a solution to one of healthcare's most overlooked challenges: the emotional toll on caregivers. As the UX Researcher and Content Writer on our team, I led the user research efforts and developed the content strategy that helped us work from September through November 2024 to develop this comprehensive solution. We recognized that while medical technology often focuses on patients, caregivers—whether family members or healthcare professionals—carry an enormous psychological burden that can lead to burnout, anxiety, and compromised care quality.

The project earned recognition as a Top 3 finalist in the Healthtech track for its innovative approach to balancing caregiver support with patient privacy and clinical effectiveness. Our solution addresses the critical need for early intervention while respecting the fundamental privacy rights that are essential in healthcare technology.

Core Challenge

How might we reduce the emotional burden on caregivers through technology while maintaining strict privacy standards and enabling early detection of health crises before they require emergency intervention?

Understanding the Problem

User Research & Problem Discovery

As the UX Researcher on the team, I led our investigation into the caregiver experience through in-depth interviews, surveys, and observational studies. My research revealed that caregiver burnout is a widespread but under-addressed issue in healthcare. Whether caring for aging parents, managing chronic conditions, or working in clinical settings, caregivers experience constant anxiety about missing critical warning signs or making wrong decisions.

Key findings from caregiver interviews and user research analysis

Key Problem Areas Identified:

  • Constant Vigilance Fatigue: Caregivers report feeling the need to be "always watching" which leads to exhaustion and anxiety
  • Fear of Missing Signs: Worry about not recognizing early symptoms of serious health episodes
  • Privacy vs. Monitoring Dilemma: Existing monitoring solutions often feel invasive to patients, creating family tension
  • Information Overload: Too much health data without clear guidance on what requires action
  • Delayed Intervention: By the time symptoms are obvious, health crises often require emergency care

Research-Driven Problem Definition

Through my content analysis of existing healthcare communications and user interviews, I identified that caregivers need peace of mind and early warning systems, but current solutions often compromise patient autonomy or overwhelm users with data they can't interpret. My research findings guided our team toward a solution that would provide meaningful insights while respecting privacy and reducing rather than increasing caregiver stress.

Our Solution & Development Process

Heroes: Privacy-First Health Monitoring

Heroes uses non-invasive monitoring technology combined with AI-powered pattern recognition to detect potential health concerns early while maintaining patient privacy and reducing caregiver anxiety through clear, actionable insights.

Rapid Ideation & Validation

Over the first phase of the project (September-October), we conducted thorough validation of our core hypothesis through interviews with healthcare workers and family caregivers. This extended timeline allowed us to identify the most critical pain points and iterate on our solution approach.

Validation process and key insights from caregiver interviews

Privacy-First Architecture Design

We developed a system architecture that processes health data locally and only surfaces insights that indicate potential concerns, rather than sharing raw personal health information. This approach maintains privacy while providing valuable early warnings.

Privacy-first architecture with local processing and encrypted insights

Content Strategy & User Communication Design

As Content Writer, I developed the communication frameworks that would make complex health information accessible to stressed caregivers. This included creating clear alert messaging, designing information hierarchies that prioritize actionable insights, and developing content that reduces anxiety rather than amplifying it.

Content strategy frameworks for anxiety-reducing health communication

Early Detection Algorithm Development

Developed pattern recognition capabilities that can identify subtle changes in routine, vitals, or behavior that might indicate developing health issues before they become critical.

Pattern recognition algorithm for early health concern detection

How It Works

Heroes combines non-invasive sensors with machine learning to create a comprehensive yet respectful monitoring solution. The system learns normal patterns for each individual and can identify deviations that might indicate health concerns, all while keeping personal data private and providing caregivers with peace of mind.

What I Learned

Leading the UX research and content strategy for this multi-month innovation challenge taught me valuable lessons about healthcare user research, the critical importance of empathy-driven content design, and how research insights can directly shape technical solutions. The September-November timeline allowed for deeper user research and more thoughtful content iteration than typical innovation projects.

Key Takeaways:

⚡

Extended development enables deeper solutions

Having September through November allowed us to conduct thorough user research and iterate on our privacy-first design, leading to a more robust and thoughtful solution.

👥

User research drives technical innovation

My interviews with caregivers revealed specific pain points that directly shaped our technical approach, proving how UX research can guide engineering decisions in healthcare technology.

🎯

Focus on the human problem, not just the technical solution

Our success came from deeply understanding caregiver anxiety and designing technology to reduce stress rather than just monitor health data.

🚀

Content strategy is crucial in healthcare UX

The way we communicate health information can either reduce or amplify caregiver anxiety. My content frameworks were essential to creating a truly supportive user experience.
